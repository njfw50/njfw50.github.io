⚙️ Canonical Engineering Laws (DOGMAs)

In Development

A personal framework of engineering principles — called DOGMAs — developed through hands-on experience building production systems. These canonical laws enforce predictable, maintainable, and secure software at every layer of development.

DOGMA 1: Explicit Over Implicit — Every behavior must be intentional and visible. No magic, no hidden side effects.
DOGMA 2: No Silent Failures — All errors must be caught, logged, and handled explicitly across every layer.
DOGMA 3: Validate ALL Inputs with Zod — Data integrity is enforced at every boundary through strict schema validation.
DOGMA 4: External Service Isolation — Third-party dependencies are wrapped in Adapters using Dependency Inversion, keeping the core system decoupled and testable.
